1 Who we are

PulseCoach is an independent Android application for heart rate variability (HRV) monitoring, training load analysis, and daily readiness scoring. It is developed and maintained by an individual developer ("we", "us", "our").

This policy describes how PulseCoach handles personal and health data. It applies to the Android application and to this privacy policy web page. No website analytics, user accounts, or server-side processing exist.

The core commitment: PulseCoach is designed as a fully on-device application. Your health data — heart rate, HRV, training load, sleep, VO₂ max estimates, GPS routes, and profile — is processed and stored on your device only. We have no server infrastructure that receives or processes your health data.

3 Permissions explained

PulseCoach requests Android permissions only when they are necessary for a specific feature. You can deny optional permissions and most functionality will still work.

BLUETOOTH_SCAN / BLUETOOTH_CONNECT Required to discover and connect to Bluetooth Low Energy HR sensors. Used only when you initiate a scan or a session. Required
ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION Required on Android ≤11 for BLE scanning (OS requirement). On Android 12+ location is not required for BLE. Also used for GPS route recording when you enable the GPS toggle during a live session. Required (≤API 30)
ACTIVITY_RECOGNITION Detects physical activity type (running, cycling, etc.) for automatic session start/stop. You can decline this — HRV and manual sessions work fully without it. Optional
FOREGROUND_SERVICE_HEALTH / LOCATION Keeps the BLE connection and GPS tracking alive while the app is in the background during a live session. The persistent notification is shown whenever this service is running. Required (for sessions)
POST_NOTIFICATIONS Used only for: (1) the daily morning HRV reminder at your chosen time, (2) the foreground service notification during live sessions, and (3) the auto-session completion alert. Optional
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM Schedules your morning HRV reminder at an exact time. Used only for the alarm you configure in Settings. Alarm feature only
Health Connect permissions (READ/WRITE) Health data is used to import historical workouts, HR, HRV, sleep, resting HR, distance, speed, calories, weight, height, and VO₂ values when you grant access. These data improve readiness, load, and trend estimates. All reads require your explicit Health Connect permission grant. User-granted
USE_BIOMETRIC / USE_FINGERPRINT Optional app lock using biometric authentication or PIN after 5 minutes of inactivity. Enabled only if you turn on App Lock in Settings. Optional
WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (API ≤28 only) Allows CSV export to the Downloads folder on Android 9 and below. Not requested on Android 10+. Export / API ≤28
INTERNET / ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE Used only for Google Drive backup (if you connect a Google account) and Garmin API sync (if configured). Not used for any analytics or tracking. Optional

4 Health Connect data use

PulseCoach integrates with Android Health Connect to read health data you have already recorded with other apps (Garmin Connect, Google Fit, Wear OS, etc.). This integration is governed by the Health Connect permissions framework.

What we read from Health Connect

  • HeartRateRecord — historical HR samples for session import
  • HeartRateVariabilityRmssdRecord — HRV readings from wearables
  • ExerciseSessionRecord — workout history for training load calculation
  • SleepSessionRecord — sleep duration for readiness scoring
  • RestingHeartRateRecord — resting HR for VO₂ estimation and mismatch detection
  • DistanceRecord, SpeedRecord, ActiveCaloriesBurnedRecord — training intensity estimation
  • WeightRecord, HeightRecord — kcal, training-load, and VO₂ computation
  • Vo2MaxRecord — VO₂ max from other apps for cross-validation

What we write to Health Connect

  • HeartRateRecord, HeartRateVariabilityRmssdRecord, Vo2MaxRecord — computed results from PulseCoach sessions when you explicitly grant write permission, available only to apps you authorize in Health Connect

Health Connect data is never sent to our servers. PulseCoach reads Health Connect data on-device and stores an app-local copy of imported sessions and derived metrics in its encrypted database. If you enable backup, that encrypted database backup may include data originally imported from Health Connect.

Limited use policy

PulseCoach's use of data obtained from Health Connect complies with the Health Connect Permissions Policy. Specifically:

  • Health Connect data is used only to provide HRV analysis, training load, and readiness features within PulseCoach.
  • Health Connect data is not used for advertising, user profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the features you explicitly use.
  • Health Connect data is not transferred to third parties, sold, or shared outside the app.
  • We do not use Health Connect data to train machine learning models or for research without your explicit consent.

5 How data is stored and encrypted

On-device database

All health data is stored in a local SQLite database encrypted with SQLCipher AES-256. The encryption key is generated randomly on first launch, stored exclusively in the Android Keystore (hardware-backed on modern devices), and never transmitted anywhere. Even if someone obtains a copy of the database file, it cannot be read without the key.

Encrypted backup files

When you enable backup, PulseCoach serialises your data and encrypts it with AES-256-GCM using a separate Keystore-backed key before writing it to Google Drive or local storage. The backup file is opaque ciphertext to anyone without your device's private key.

App lock

If you enable App Lock in Settings, the app requires biometric authentication (fingerprint, face) or your device PIN after 5 minutes of inactivity. This uses Android BiometricPrompt and does not transmit any biometric data.

Network security

All outbound network connections to Google Drive, Garmin, and weather services use HTTPS/TLS. The Android Network Security configuration blocks cleartext HTTP traffic. PulseCoach does not currently use certificate pinning.

No PulseCoach cloud database. PulseCoach has no backend server, no PulseCoach user accounts, and no cloud database operated by us. Optional network features are encrypted backup to your Google Drive AppData folder, Garmin import if configured, weather lookup, and files you explicitly export or share.

6 Health and wellness disclaimer

PulseCoach is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice. The app is for personal wellness, fitness awareness, and trend tracking only. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, prevent, or monitor any disease or medical condition.

Readiness scores, HRV metrics, VO₂ estimates, training load, calorie estimates, threshold guidance, and coaching notifications are algorithmic estimates. They can be wrong because sensors, imports, profile values, GPS, power meters, and physiological assumptions can be wrong.

  • Do not use PulseCoach for emergency, clinical, occupational, aviation, life-support, or safety-critical decisions.
  • Do not ignore symptoms, pain, dizziness, chest discomfort, unusual breathlessness, or medical advice because of an app score.
  • VO₂ estimates are not laboratory measurements. Calculated VO₂ depends materially on resting HR, max HR, weight, GPS or power quality, and steady-state effort quality.
  • External device VO₂ values from Garmin, FIT files, or Health Connect are displayed as device estimates and may use proprietary methods outside PulseCoach's control.
  • Training recommendations are not personalized prescriptions from a physician, certified coach, or sports scientist.

If you have a medical condition, are pregnant, are returning from illness or injury, take heart-rate-altering medication, or are unsure whether exercise is safe for you, consult a qualified healthcare professional before relying on the app.

7 Backup and cloud sync

Backup is disabled by default and requires your explicit opt-in in Settings.

Google Drive backup (optional)

If you connect a Google account, PulseCoach can back up an encrypted copy of your database to your Google Drive's "App Data" folder. This folder:

  • Is accessible only to PulseCoach — no other app and no human (including us) can read it
  • Contains only the encrypted PulseCoach backup file. The backup can include sessions or metrics that PulseCoach previously imported from Health Connect, Garmin, or files.
  • Is governed by Google's Privacy Policy

We use only the drive.appdata OAuth scope — we cannot read any other files in your Google Drive.

Local backup (optional)

You can export an encrypted backup file to your device's Downloads folder (or app-private external storage on Android 10+). This file is the same AES-256-GCM encrypted payload as the Drive backup.

Android automatic backup

The app sets android:allowBackup="false", which means Android's automatic cloud backup of app data is disabled. This prevents unencrypted data from being uploaded to Google's servers without your knowledge.

8 Data sharing and user-controlled transfers

We do not sell, rent, or disclose your personal or health data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or data-broker purposes. There are no advertising partners, data brokers, analytics providers, or third-party telemetry SDKs in PulseCoach.

The only scenarios where data leaves your device or is made available outside PulseCoach are user-controlled feature flows:

  • Google Drive backup — encrypted, opt-in, stored in your own account's AppData folder, inaccessible to us
  • Health Connect writes — computed results written back to Health Connect, visible only to apps you explicitly authorise on your device
  • Garmin API — if you configure Garmin credentials in Settings, activity data is fetched from Garmin's servers using your credentials. No PulseCoach-generated health data is sent to Garmin by PulseCoach.
  • Weather lookup — if route data is present, approximate grid coordinates and session time may be used to fetch historical weather from Open-Meteo. This is used for workout context, not tracking.
  • Exports and share sheets — data you explicitly export to GPX/CSV/debug ZIP or share through Android's system share sheet

9 Third-party services

Google Play Services / Google Sign-In

Used only for Google account authentication when you connect Google Drive for backup. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Android Health Connect

An Android platform service provided by Google. Data interactions are governed by the Health Connect Permissions Policy and your Health Connect permissions settings.

Garmin Connect API (optional)

If you configure Garmin Connect in Settings, the app makes HTTPS requests to Garmin's public API using OAuth 1.0a credentials you supply. This is entirely optional and can be left unconfigured. Governed by Garmin's Privacy Policy.

Open-Meteo weather API

Used only to fetch historical weather context for sessions with route data. PulseCoach rounds location to a coarse grid before lookup and does not use this service for analytics, advertising, or user tracking. Governed by Open-Meteo's terms.

Open-source libraries

PulseCoach uses open-source Android libraries (Room, WorkManager, MPAndroidChart, SQLCipher, OkHttp, etc.) that operate entirely on-device. None of these libraries collect or transmit any data.

No advertising SDKs. PulseCoach contains no advertising SDK, no Facebook SDK, no Firebase Analytics, no Crashlytics, and no equivalent third-party telemetry library.

10 Data retention and deletion

On-device retention

  • All data is stored on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app.
  • PulseCoach automatically prunes derived metric rows (training load, readiness scores) older than 180 days to keep the database efficient.
  • Raw HR samples, RR intervals, GPS points, and session records are retained indefinitely unless you delete them from the History screen.

Deleting your data

  • Individual sessions: Delete any session in History. Associated HR, RR, GPS, power, HRV, and derived metrics are deleted with the session.
  • All data: Uninstalling PulseCoach from your device removes the encrypted database. Data written to Health Connect remains in Health Connect and can be managed there.
  • Backup data: Delete backup files from Google Drive AppData or your local backup location. Creating a new backup may replace or prune older PulseCoach backup files, but you remain responsible for deleting copies you exported or shared.
  • Health Connect data written by PulseCoach: Open the Health Connect app on your device → Settings → App Permissions → PulseCoach → Remove access. This removes PulseCoach's writes.

11 Children's privacy

PulseCoach is designed for adult athletes and fitness enthusiasts. The app is not directed at children under 16 years of age, and we do not knowingly collect health or personal data from children under 16.

If you believe a child under 16 is using PulseCoach, please contact us using the details in Section 14. We will assist in removing the data.

The minimum age guard in the profile (age must be in the range 15–90 to be accepted) reflects this restriction — users outside this range default to a population average value and receive an in-app reminder to set their age.

12 Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights regarding your personal data. Because all data is stored locally on your device and we have no central server with your data, most rights are exercised directly through the app.

Under GDPR (EU/EEA/UK)

  • Right of access — use CSV Export in Settings to export all your data
  • Right to erasure — delete sessions in History, remove backups you created, revoke Health Connect access, or uninstall the app
  • Right to portability — CSV export provides your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to rectification — edit your profile in Settings at any time
  • Right to object to automated decision-making — readiness scores are computed locally on your device; you can disable them by revoking Health Connect permissions

Under CCPA (California)

PulseCoach does not sell personal information. California residents have the right to know what data is collected (see Section 2) and to delete it (see Section 10).

Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

Where GDPR applies, the legal basis for processing is performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b)) — you request HRV analysis and we perform it on your device — and, for health data under Article 9, explicit consent obtained through the Health Connect permission dialog and the first-run consent screen.

13 Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when we add new features, change data practices, or when required by law. Material changes will be communicated via:

  • An in-app notification on the next app launch after the change
  • A version bump of the policy with an updated "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • A commit to this GitHub repository with a clear description of what changed

Continued use of PulseCoach after a material change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not accept the changes, you may stop using the app and delete your data as described in Section 10.
